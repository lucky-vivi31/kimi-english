Hi! My name is Ben, and I love cookies! Last weekend, my sister Lucy and I decided to bake chocolate chip cookies for our family. We thought it would be fun to work together and learn about the science of baking. What we discovered was not only delicious but also very interesting!

Every time we bake, we cooperate as a team. I measure the flour, sugar, and butter while Lucy mixes all the ingredients together. Do you know what happens when we mix these ingredients? It's like a chemical reaction! The butter and sugar combine to create a smooth mixture. When we add baking soda, it helps the cookies become fluffy and soft. The heat from the oven transforms the soft dough into crispy, golden cookies. Science is everywhere in baking!

Last Saturday, something funny happened! We forgot to set the timer, and our first batch of cookies burned in the oven! They turned completely black and smelled terrible. At first, we felt disappointed. But instead of giving up, we laughed about it and tried again. This time, we set the timer for exactly 12 minutes and watched the cookies carefully. When they came out perfect and golden brown, we were so happy! We learned that working together and not giving up is very important.

After the cookies cooled down, we shared them with our whole family. Mom, Dad, and our little brother all enjoyed the cookies we made. Seeing everyone smile made us feel proud and happy. Sharing what we create with others brings so much joy!

From now on, Lucy and I will bake cookies together every month. We will try new recipes and experiment with different flavors. Maybe we will even learn to make other desserts too! What about you? Have you ever baked something with your family? What did you learn about cooperation and sharing?
