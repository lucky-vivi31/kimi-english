Hi! My name is Sam. One rainy afternoon, I was sitting by the window watching the raindrops slide down the glass. Suddenly, a big question popped into my head: where does all this rain actually come from? I grabbed my science notebook and decided to find out. That question led me to discover one of nature's most incredible secrets – the water cycle! 

The water cycle is a continuous journey that water takes every single day. First, the sun heats water in oceans, rivers, and lakes. This causes the water to evaporate – it turns into invisible water vapor that rises into the sky. High up in the atmosphere, the vapor cools down and condenses into tiny water droplets, which form clouds. When the clouds collect enough droplets, the water becomes too heavy to stay up and falls back down as rain or snow! This process repeats over and over, forever. 

Last Tuesday, my science teacher showed us a cool experiment in class. She poured hot water into a jar and placed a plate of ice on top. We watched as steam rose from the hot water and hit the cold plate, forming little water droplets that dripped back down. It was like a tiny rain cloud inside a jar! The whole class gasped in amazement. That experiment helped me understand the water cycle in a very real way. 

Now I feel so grateful for rain! Every drop of water that falls from the sky has traveled on an incredible journey. Without the water cycle, there would be no rivers, no rain, and no life on Earth. Water is truly one of our most precious resources. My curiosity about one rainy afternoon turned into a deep love for science! 

From now on, whenever it rains, I will think about this amazing journey water has taken. I will also try to save water every day because every drop is precious. What about you? What questions about nature make you curious? Have you ever wondered where something in nature comes from?
